Why Syncr

Rich presence, done right

Everything you'd expect from a desktop Rich Presence app, running quietly from your browser.

Real-time updates

Titles, artwork, progress bars, and quick links back to what you're doing, updating live as you browse.

No Discord login

Syncr talks directly to the Discord desktop app on your machine. It never asks for your Discord password or token.

Nothing leaves your PC

No cloud backend. Page data goes Firefox → a small local app → Discord. Nothing is sent to a Syncr server, ever.

You control everything

Open the popup and toggle any site on or off. Only the activities you enable ever show up on your profile.

Auto-updating activities

New activities appear automatically. Presence files hot-update from GitHub. No full reinstall to get the latest.

Free & open source

No paywalls, no premium tier, no locked features. The whole thing is on GitHub. Add your own sites if you like.

How it works

Two small pieces, fully local

Your page never talks to a server we run. The whole chain lives on your machine.

Firefox tab

You browse a supported site like you normally would.

Content script

A tiny script reads just the fields needed for presence, right there in the page.

Local host app

A small native app on your PC maps it to a Discord activity, then hands it to the Discord desktop app over local IPC. No login, no token.

Discord profile

Your friends see it live, exactly like a desktop app would show it.

Privacy by design

Built to stay on your machine

Rich presence should not cost you your data. Syncr is local-first from the ground up, and the code is right there for you to check.

  • No Discord account loginTalks straight to the Discord desktop app locally. Never handles your credentials.
  • No cloud backendNothing is uploaded to a Syncr server. There isn't one. The data path is entirely on your PC.
  • You choose what to shareDisable any activity you don't want on your profile with a single toggle.
  • Privacy-conscious activitiesSensitive sites like email use deliberately generic labels and never expose details.
